## release-agent
Nelson Cat Cafe refinement cycle 1. Three concrete issues found and fixed:

1. ERROR PROPAGATION BUG: A correction identified in viability-assessment.html (CBT grants not applicable to private for-profit businesses) was never applied back to the source document (feasibility.html). Risk #5 in feasibility.html continued to recommend Columbia Basin Trust as a capital pathway. Always check that corrections flagged in one artifact are reflected in the source artifacts that contained the error — not just in the correction document.

2. MISSING ARTIFACT: release-brief.html listed investor-pitch.html in its artifacts table, but the file had never been created. The artifact manifest was empty for all phases. When a release-brief references a specific artifact by filename, verify the file actually exists on the blackboard before marking the pipeline complete. Created investor-pitch.html this cycle.

3. FLAT AVERAGES HIDE SEASONAL RISK: All revenue scenarios used a flat monthly average that obscured the real cash flow risk profile. For a Nelson BC tourism business, April and October are structurally dangerous months with margins near zero. Adding a seasonality table (Section 3A in feasibility.html) that models revenue by month surfaced a ~$70K gap between the flat-average model ($383K) and the seasonal model ($313K). This also prompted an upward revision of the working capital recommendation from 3 months to 4 months. For any tourism-dependent business in a seasonal market, always model monthly, not annually.
