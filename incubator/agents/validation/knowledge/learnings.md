
## validation
Vancouver Dinner — Cycle 3 Validation Learnings:

1. SOURCE ARTIFACT HYGIENE: When a correction is made in a validation report, the original source artifact (research.html) is not automatically updated. After three cycles, research.html still showed a fabricated "Night in New Orleans" event at Brix & Mortar as confirmed fact. Future validation cycles should explicitly check whether prior corrections propagated back to all source documents, not just the primary deliverable.

2. ADDRESS CROSS-CHECKING: Brix & Mortar had two different addresses across documents ("1138 Homer St" in research.html vs "1137 Hamilton St" in validation report). These are different streets in Yaletown. When addresses appear across multiple artifacts, compare them explicitly — street name differences (Homer vs Hamilton) are easy to miss.

3. CERTIFICATION VS PRACTICE: "Halal-acceptable" (vegan kitchen, no meat/alcohol) is not the same as "halal-certified." For MeeT Yaletown, prior cycles marked it as "Yes (halal)" which could mislead a strictly observant guest. Always distinguish between practical acceptability and formal certification for dietary/religious requirements.

4. PARKING COSTS: Downtown Vancouver evening commercial parking in 2026 runs $15–25+ for 3–4 hours. The $10–20 estimate in prior cycles was outdated. Always apply a cost-of-living adjustment for major urban centers when estimating parking/transport costs.

5. EDGE CASE COVERAGE: All scripts and templates were written for 8–10 guests. No guidance existed for the 5-person minimum scenario, where group booking policies (deposits, CC holds, auto-gratuity) typically don't apply. Cover both ends of the stated range in any action guide.

6. CALENDAR VERIFICATION: March 27, 2026 is a Friday — confirmed by calculation (March 1, 2026 = Sunday; +26 days = Friday). Always verify day-of-week claims for event planning research rather than assuming the idea statement is correct.

7. CANUCKS SCHEDULE: Verifying no home game conflict is a genuine value-add for Vancouver dinner planning. Rogers Arena is in downtown Vancouver; game nights add measurable pressure to restaurants, transit, and parking. This check should be standard for any Vancouver event planning task.
