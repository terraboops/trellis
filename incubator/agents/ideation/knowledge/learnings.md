## Get Baked & Watch The Matrix — 4-Cycle Validation Learnings

### Idea Summary
Cannabis-friendly trans rights fundraiser film screening of The Matrix (confirmed trans allegory) in Nelson BC, Canada. Raises money via door donations, raffle (dispensary-donated prizes), cash sponsorships, merch, and pre-event online donations.

### Verified Facts (4 cycles of research)
1. **The Matrix IS a confirmed trans allegory** — Lilly Wachowski confirmed in 2020 interview
2. **Criterion Pictures Canada** = correct vendor for Warner Bros. non-theatrical Canadian film licenses (NOT Swank, which is US). Call 1-800-565-1996. Cost $150-450 CAD for <200 attendees.
3. **BC Cannabis law**: No indoor smoking/vaping at events with staff. "Arrive elevated" framing is legally correct and culturally authentic. Outdoor venues (Gyro Park) allow on-site consumption.
4. **Nelson Pride founded 1996** — first Pride outside a major city in North America. 2026 = 30th anniversary. Pride Week typically late August.
5. **Nelson dispensaries**: Woody Nelson, The Potorium, Buddy's Place — all downtown, community-minded.
6. **BC Gaming raffle license**: Partner with established nonprofit (Nelson Pride Society) — organizers cannot apply directly without 12+ months operating history. Class B license = $25 fee, 10 business days.
7. **Nano Banana**: REAL tool — Google Gemini 2.5 Flash Image model. Available at nanobanana.art, nano-banana.ai, Pixlr, OpenArt, ImagineArt. Free credits available.

### Lessons for Future Similar Ideas
- Cannabis event regulations vary widely by jurisdiction — always verify indoor consumption rules first
- Film licensing in Canada goes through different vendors than US (Criterion vs Swank)
- Community fundraiser raffle licenses typically require an established nonprofit partner
- "Arrive elevated" is legally correct framing for indoor cannabis events in BC
- Split beneficiary (national org + local org) tells a better story than either alone
- Social media content calendar is always a missing deliverable — create it proactively
- Comparable events validation is critical: always search for precedents to calibrate revenue projections
- Nelson BC is unusually progressive for its size — community engagement will be strong
