  <section>
    <div class="section-label">Practical Tips</div>
    <div class="tips-grid">
      <div class="tip-card">
        <h4>Headcount discipline</h4>
        <p>Most restaurants charge a per-head no-show fee for large groups. Get a firm commitment from each guest before giving the restaurant a number. Eight confirmed is better than ten with two maybes.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="tip-card">
        <h4>Arrival time</h4>
        <p>Friday nights in downtown Vancouver are busy. Book 6:30 or 7:00 PM to avoid the post-work rush at 6:00 PM. Tell guests to arrive by 6:20 so you are all seated together on time.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="tip-card">
        <h4>Transit tip for guests</h4>
        <p>Share station and walk directions with each guest when you confirm. "Take Expo Line to Stadium-Chinatown, then walk south on Beatty for 4 minutes" is all anyone needs. Compass Card works on all TransLink services.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="tip-card">
        <h4>Communicate dietary needs now</h4>
        <p>Ask guests for restrictions today, not the day before. If anyone has a severe allergy (nut, shellfish, dairy), tell the restaurant at booking and repeat it on the confirmation call. Saying "I'm allergic to tree nuts and it can be serious" is more actionable than a vague note.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="tip-card">
        <h4>Splitting the bill</h4>
        <p>Ask when you book whether they can do multiple bill splits. Most downtown Vancouver restaurants split up to 6 ways without trouble. More than that requires advance notice. Mention it when you call so there's no awkward conversation at the end of the night.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="tip-card">
        <h4>Backup plan</h4>
        <p>Book your first choice today. If that falls through close to the date, CRAFT Beer Market is the most forgiving walk-in option for a group this size &mdash; they handle large parties without reservations better than the rest.</p>
      </div>
    </div>
